04 · Mudras with Prem · Client Project

The site had traffic.
People just weren't converting.

My job was to figure out why and fix it.

The brief

Dr. Premlata Haralalka has been teaching Mudras, Reiki, and Pranic Healing for 25 years. 50,000+ students across 15 countries. Someone I worked with at my Tata Digital internship asked me to help redesign their website.The site had traffic but not enought people were booking courses.

The diagnosis

First thing I did was map the session flow before touching anything. Where people were entering, where they were dropping, what they were doing in between. That told me which pages actually mattered and where to focus first.

58% of sessions were starting on the homepage but most visitors were detouring to the About page before ever looking at Courses. This was hurting conversions. People were actively searching for a reason to trust the instructor before they'd consider buying. The homepage wasn't giving it to them.

The second problem was navigation. Visitors couldn't easily find or understand what courses were on offer.

Two problems. Both fixable.

What I did
  • Pulled session flow data from Wix Analytics to map exactly where visitors were dropping and why before touching anything
  • Used AI to rapidly prototype 3 homepage concepts — what normally takes a designer weeks took hours. The client could see real options and make a decision instead of reacting to one concept
  • Rebuilt the homepage hierarchy — testimonials and instructor credibility moved above the fold to address the trust deficit before anything else
  • Rewrote key headlines and CTAs to speak directly to someone who'd never heard of Mudras or Pranic Healing. The old copy assumed familiarity. Most visitors didn't have it
  • Analysed 200+ visitor enquiries using AI to find the 5 most common questions, then turned them into an FAQ section on the page. Objections addressed before they became drop-offs
  • Redesigned course navigation so visitors could find and understand every offering without friction

What I learned

The biggest learning was how much the data already tells you if you actually look at it. The About page detour was sitting right there in the session flow. That one insight, that people needed trust signals before they'd look at anything else, shaped everything I did on the homepage.

The other thing that stuck with me was how fast AI let me move on the design side. Having multiple homepage concepts ready in minutes meant I wasn't going back and forth on layouts for weeks. It freed me up to actually focus on the growth side of things, building out email automations, thinking through the full funnel, figuring out what happens after someone lands on the page.
