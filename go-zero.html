03 · Go Zero · AI Ad

Most AI Ads get hate comments. Mine went viral

Here's the secret.

The brief

Go Zero (D2C ice cream brand) ran a competition for AI-generated ads on LinkedIn. I entered. My submission got the highest views of all entries.

View original post ↗
The insight

Most AI ads fail the same way. Technically impressive, emotionally empty. You watch them and you can tell something's off.

I didn't want to make an ad like that.

Instead of starting with a prompt, I started with a memory. Almost every Indian person has the same one: summers at your grandmother's house, mangoes, that specific kind of heat and slowness. I tried to capture that in an ad.

I produced the entire thing on Google Veo3. No crew, no shoot, no budget.

What I did
  • I researched Go Zero's existing content and identified the gap. They talk a lot about guilt-free but never about nostalgia.
  • Then I looked at how leading health brands actually market themselves. Diet Coke was the biggest reference. They never make it feel like a sacrifice, they just talk about the taste.
  • I decided to build the concept around a universal Indian summer memory rather than product features.
  • Iterated on scenes until the emotional tone felt right, not just visually correct.

What I learned

AI can generate anything now. But that's also the problem. It leads to technically perfect ads that feel soulless. What breaks that is starting with something real. A memory, an emotion, something people can actually relate to.
