I Built a 15L Brand from my bedroom

Here's how it happened.

The origin

When I was 17, I ran an F1 themed Instagram page. One interesting thing I noticed was that while running the page, I observed that F1 was growing fast in India, but good merchandise was nearly impossible to find without paying a premium for imports. Everything was either overpriced or cheap fan merch.

I had ₹20,000 saved from freelancing. So I decided to go all in and make my own.

What I did

The budget basically wrote the strategy.

  • Most manufacturers I approached had huge MOQs, so I used print-on-demand. No inventory, no dead stock, no upfront risk
  • Once there was consistent demand, I shifted to bulk inventory model to get the cost advantage.
  • Since traditional photoshoots were too expensive, . I taught myself how to make hyper-realistic AI mockups and ran them as performance creatives on Meta. CTRs consistently came in at 1.5-2%
  • Scaled Meta ads to ₹2.79L in total spend at ~4.5X ROAS, managing the full P&L myself — sourcing, pricing, margins, ad spend allocation
  • Once I had capital, I started seeding product to micro-influencers. Their UGC went straight back into paid and lifted ROAS by ~30%

What I learned

Most of what I learned came from working with what I had. No shoot budget meant figuring out AI creatives. No agency meant learning Meta from scratch, managing spend, reading what worked and what didn't.

Doing everything yourself forces you to understand how it all connects.

The one thing I'd do differently is not rely so heavily on performance marketing. When everything runs through Meta, your entire business depends on an algorithm you have no control over. I'd invest more in retention, community, offline activations, things that don't stop working the moment you pause spend.
