02 · Rapid Racing · F1 Instagram Page

20,000 followers. 9 months. ₹0 spent.

Here's what actually grew it.

The origin

I started Rapid Racing when I was 17. I'd just gotten into F1 and thought why not make a page about it. I'd be lying if I said I had a content strategy.

But as I started posting and the numbers started moving, I decided to actually focus on growing it.

What I did
  • Posted 3 times a day consistently. The feedback loop was so short I could tell within hours what was working and what wasn't.
  • While I started with memes, I pivoted into long-form carousel posts. In 2020-21, Instagram was pushing them hard. A long slide post could keep someone on the app for 2-3 minutes, which the algorithm rewarded with increased reach. So I started doing race breakdowns, historical stories, latest news.
  • Grew a primarily western audience across US, UK and Europe. F1 naturally attracts a higher-income demographic, which made the page commercially valuable beyond just the follower count.

What I learned

Posting three times a day was the best growth education I've had. The feedback loop was so short that I started to understand which ideas were worth 500 likes and which were worth 10,000. You develop a feel for what will spread before you even post it.

The other lesson was finding formats nobody in your niche is doing and doubling down on them. Nobody in the F1 space was doing long-form breakdowns. So when people saw them, a huge percentage ended up following..
